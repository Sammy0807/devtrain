About Us

Our Background.

DEVTRAIN - COMMUNITY AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP DEVELOPMENT INITIATIVE started as Hephzibah Missions in April 2014 as a youth based social club and finally metamorphosed into a non-for-profit organization now called DEVTRAIN. The club which comprised of young people was established with a keen desire to assist and equip youths with innovative ideas to excel academically. Realizing that youths have very close ties with their parents in Nigeria it became expedient to include women and men on the programmes of the club.

Our Vision.

The vision of DEVTRAIN is to AFFECT CITIZENS LIVES POSITIVELY ensuring there is equal distribution of available resources for all and RAISING AGENTS OF CHANGE through human capacity development and knowledge transfer.

Our Mission.

  1. To equip communities to thrive and flourish through innovative ideas for growth and development.
  2. To costantly and vehemently approach the capacity building of youths to enhance their skills and talents
  3. To deploy all resources through our various activities to ensure learning, unlearning and relearning for the underserved and unreached youths in our society.

What We Do.

DevTrain supports communities to identify and meet needs through different rights based projects and livelihoods improvement activities/programmes to promote entrepreneurship, good health, education for all, empowerment and sustainable livelihoods of rural, semi-urban and urban communities to improve the quality of life of community members across Abuja and Benue State in Nigeria. We transfer knowledge and skills to passionate youths to equip them carry out DevTrain activities across Nigeria. Our approach encourages a wider perspective on participatory leadership and better understand mentorship.

23 LGA wide MyPVCMyPOWER campaign in Benue State

INTRODUCTION
It is alarming the rate at which the Benue populaces have over the years left the decision of the leadership of the state in the hands of a few individuals. The passivity and high level illiteracy among the electorate in Benue State is one that needs to be addressed. A large percentage of the people in the state especially in rural communities and sadly even in the state capital, Makurdi does not have a Permanent Voters Card (PVC) and at such are not eligible to vote.

In an online MyPVCMyPOWER Survey conducted by Project Beautiful BENUE in collaboration with iVoteNigeria (2018) a sample size of 240 participants responded in the survey and it was discovered that 28.57% of respondent have Temporary Voters Card, 33.33% have the Permanent Voters Card while 38.10% of respondents do not have neither TVC nor PVC.

Also from the analysis it was discovered that 11.11% respondents lost their TVC, 11.11% lost their PVC and 77.78% of respondents encountered challenges trying to participate in the ongoing Continuous Voters registration.

Thus, the need to enlighten and encourage the Nigerian citizens to participate in the ongoing continuous voter's registered.

It is against this background that Project Beautiful BENUE and iVoteNigeria is carrying out this voter's sensitization campaign to reach the youth, women, traders and people living with disability and albinos. The approach is to reach everyone with information on the power of the PVC and facilitate them on how to obtain PVC. Moreso this campaign shall address some of the misconceptions about electoral processes and introduce novel, positive and truthful ideas of the soundness of the electoral process will be introduced into the minds thus restoring a new mindset and perceptions in the electoral process in Nigeria and particularly Benue.

About

MyPVCMyPOWER is a co-founded project of PROJECT BEAUTIFUL BENUE AND DEVTRAIN - COMMUNITY AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP DEVELOPMENT INITIATIVE the campaign is a strategic awareness campaign targeted at young girls/boys, women, men, People Living with Disability, Albinos etc with the aim of informing and facilitating them to participate in the Continuous Voters Registration, obtain a Permanent Voters Card and participate in electoral process.

Organization Background

Project Beautiful BENUE a brain child of passion started in March 2016 as just a gathering of young friends who were zealous to contribute to their society positively and by extension, Africa. Over the last two years, it has grown into a more organized movement and an organization with key focus on education, character development, capacity building and empowerment.

DEVTRAIN - COMMUNITY AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP DEVELOPMENT INITIATIVE a youth based social club and finally metamorphosed into a non-for-profit organization. DevTrain is a Non- Governmental Organization involved in social entrepreneurship, integrated community development activities.

DevTrain supports communities to identify and meet needs through different rights based projects and livelihoods improvement activities/programmes to promote entrepreneurship, good health, education for all, empowerment and sustainable livelihoods of rural, semi-urban and urban communities to improve the quality of life of community members across Abuja and Benue State.

OBJECTIVES
  1. To raise awareness on the need to register (voters registration) amongst youth, women, men and PLWD
  2. To facilitate the acquisition of Permanent Voters Card in the next voters registration exercise by end of 2018
  3. To promote a wider youth, women, men and PLWD participation and involvement in electoral process by 2019
  4. To sensitize youths and women on the dangers of election violence
  5. To sensitize and mobilize youth, women, men and PLWD especially women’s voice in decision-making, peace-building, leadership and governance
outcomes
  1. Increased number of voters (particularly youths and women)
  2. Increased awareness on electoral process
  3. Increased inclusive participation and involvement of Nigerian citizens in electoral process
  4. Decreased election violence and on-rest during elections
  5. Increased women’s voice in decision-making, peace-building, leadership and governance

CAMPAIGN METHODOLOGY
The Voter’s awareness campaign tagged “MyPVCMyPOWER campaign” targeting adults deployed the use of participatory and experiential learning approach. The facilitators ensured that participants were carried along at every stage of the campaign. The facilitators also used local languages as appropriate and it was both participatory and there were questions and answers sessions. The team has successfully reached.

Awareness was created on the need for Nigerian citizens (young girls/boys, women, men, People Living with Disability, Albinos etc) to participate in the ongoing Continuous Voters Registration, obtain a Permanent Voters Card and participate in electoral process. This project approach and methodologies was in furtherance of our strategic mission aimed at promoting inclusion, reaching young people and the un-reached across the LGAs in Benue to participate in electoral processes.

So far these outreaches have been conducted in 38 council wards in Makurdi, Konshisha, Vandeikya, Gwer West, Gboko and Kwande LGAs of Benue State reaching a total of 45,342 (28300F, 17042M)

These campaigns deployed both one on one, house to house, market to market and group sensitization approach. The outcome of the meetings disclosed that eligible voters encountered a lot of challenges such as:

  1. Attitude of INEC staff during registration process.
  2. Denial access to participate in the voters registration due to lack of means of identification.
  3. Market women complained of wasting so much time waiting at the Registration Centre’s.
  4. Most young people they showed no concern in the ongoing CVR.
  5. Most community members complained of conducting previous registration in different locations and how they cannot travel back to obtain their cards.
